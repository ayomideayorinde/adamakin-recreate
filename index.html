What Does Ratification Mean?

Ratification means both you and the seller have officially agreed to all the terms of the purchase contract. It’s like a formal handshake saying you’re both on the same page. This is a key step before you can move forward with things like inspections and closing the deal.

What this for you:

  • Legally Binding Agreement: Ratification turns the initial offer and counteroffers into a legally binding contract. This protects both you and the seller.
  • Security and Certainty: Ratification provides security for both parties. You, the buyer, can now confidently move forward with things like scheduling inspections and securing financing, knowing the seller can’t back out without facing potential repercussions.

What Is A Deed Of Assignment?

A deed of assignment is a legal document that serves as evidence of a real estate transaction, outlining the transfer of ownership between the seller(s) and buyer(s) of a property. It’s a crucial contract in real estate acquisitions, including:

  • Outright buying of land or property
  • Installment payments formula of purchase
  • Lease of land or property
The Deed of Assignment fee for Lagos State land is 3% of the value of the property. For more information on the fees for Lagos State land, you can visit the Lagos State Government website. You can also contact the Lagos State Government for the most up-to-date information on land fees.
